# Voeli — Emotional Intent of Untranslatable Phrases

What is the speaker actually *trying to do* when they say each phrase? Not the dictionary meaning — the emotional move. Useful for the app when there's no direct translation: instead of failing, the AI can **explain the feeling**.

Format: **Phrase** — what they're really doing emotionally / what reaction they want.

---

## ENGLISH → no Vietnamese equivalent

### Gen X

- **Cowabunga** — Pure hype release. Wants you to share their excitement, no irony.
- **Totally tubular / Tubular** — Sarcastic-tinged enthusiasm. Says "this is great" with a wink at how dated they sound.
- **Bodacious** — Admiration, slightly leering. Wants to register that someone/something is impressive without being too direct.
- **Gnarly** — Adrenaline-share. Reporting something extreme and inviting you to be impressed or grossed out.
- **Fer sure / Like, totally** — Performative agreement. Less about agreeing, more about signaling group identity (Valley Girl tribe).
- **Gag me with a spoon / Grody to the max** — Theatrical disgust. Wants laughs from how over-the-top the rejection sounds.
- **Eat my shorts** — Affectionate dismissal. Says "go away" but really means "I'm not actually mad, I'm being playful."
- **Don't have a cow** — De-escalation. "Calm down, you're overreacting" — but softer than telling someone to calm down.
- **Headbanger / Yuppie** — Identity labels. Names a tribe you belong to or look down on. Emotional move = social positioning.
- **Word to your mother** — Earnest oath, with a smile. Wants you to know they're serious without sounding heavy.
- **Take a chill pill** — Same as "don't have a cow" — softens "calm down" into a joke.

### Millennials

- **Bye Felicia** — Dismissal that signals you're done. Used when you've decided someone isn't worth the energy. Wants the listener to register *finality*.
- **Yasss** — Pure validation amplification. Stretching a "yes" into "yes I see you, I support you, go off." Wants the other person to feel cheered on.
- **Werk** — Same as "yasss" but adds an admiration of effort. "I see how hard you're trying and you're killing it."
- **Cray / Obvi / Perf / Totes** — Casualness signal. The shortening *itself* is the message: "I'm being chill, this isn't a big deal."
- **Netflix and chill** — Innuendo with plausible deniability. Wants intimacy without the awkwardness of asking directly.
- **Eboy / Egirl** — Aesthetic identity marker. Wants to claim membership in an emo/alt internet tribe.
- **Roman Empire** — Self-deprecating obsession. Admitting you think about something way too much. Wants laughs and recognition ("oh same!").
- **Villain era** — Permission to be selfish. Announcing "I'm not being nice anymore" but framing it as a phase, not a personality flaw.
- **Main character** — Self-mythologizing. Treating your life as a story worth being the lead in. Wants to feel significant.
- **Soft life** — Aspirational rest. Rejecting hustle culture. Wants comfort and ease as the goal, not achievement.
- **Therapy speak** — Vocabulary borrowing for self-protection. Names emotional dynamics so the speaker doesn't have to feel them as raw.
- **Trauma dump** — Self-aware oversharing label. Acknowledges they're putting too much on you. Half-apology, half-permission to do it anyway.
- **Gaslighting** — Naming manipulation. Wants the listener to recognize a *pattern*, not just one bad moment.
- **Micro-aggression** — Naming subtle harm. Wants the small thing taken seriously.
- **Quiet quitting** — Naming a refusal. Marks a stance ("I'm not playing your work game anymore") without confrontation.
- **Touch base / Circle back** — Friendliness as armor. Corporate softness that wants to seem warm while keeping things transactional.
- **Breadcrumbing / Benching / Orbiting** — Naming a romantic injury. Wants the listener to see a *strategy* in someone else's behavior.
- **Situationship** — Refusing to define. Wants to acknowledge a romantic connection without committing to "relationship."

### Gen Z

- **Skibidi / Gyatt / Mewing** — Inside-baseball signaling. Saying these proves you're online enough. Wants tribal recognition.
- **Sigma** — Self-mythology with an ironic edge. Half-believes the lone-wolf identity, half-jokes about it.
- **Looksmaxxing** — Optimization framing applied to self. Names the project of becoming more attractive as if it's an engineering problem. Wants to depersonalize the vulnerability of caring how you look.
- **NPC** — Dismissing someone as scripted/boring. Wants to feel above them.
- **Caught in 4K** — Triumphant exposure. Wants to register both that you caught them AND that there's evidence.
- **404 coded** — Pity dismissal. "They don't get it and never will."
- **Touch grass** — Affectionate insult. Says "you've been online too long" with care underneath.
- **Sneaky link** — Naming intimacy with built-in secrecy. Wants the thrill of doing something hidden.
- **Demure / Brat** — Performance mode declaration. Announcing how they're acting today.
- **Girlboss / That girl / Mother** — Aspirational labels with shifting irony. "Mother" = ultimate compliment; "Girlboss" can be sincere or mocking.
- **Roman Empire / Villain era / Main character / Side character** — Self-narrating life as a movie. Wants the moment to feel meaningful, not random.
- **Gatekeep** — Naming withholding. Wants to call out someone hoarding access or info.
- **Soft launch / Hard launch** — Controlled relationship reveal. Wants to manage what others know about your love life *as content*.
- **L / W / Big L / Big W** — Score-keeping. Reduces life events to gaming wins/losses. Wants emotional distance from outcomes.
- **Ratio** — Public scoring of disagreement. Says "you're so wrong even the algorithm rejects you."
- **Ick / Caught an ick / Ick list** — Naming sudden involuntary disgust. Wants the listener to know the disgust wasn't chosen — it just happened.
- **Sus** — Suspicion lite. Lighter than "I don't trust them" — playful paranoia.
- **Yeet** — Action + sound effect. Wants the comedy of physical force narrated.
- **Sheesh** — Awe with attitude. Both impressed and mocking at the same time.
- **Bruh** — Disbelief shortcut. Single-word reaction that says "I cannot."
- **Bestie / Twin / Unc / Zaddy** — Affectionate role assignment. Calling someone "bestie" claims them; "twin" claims similarity; "unc" softens condescension; "zaddy" is hot-older-guy admiration.
- **Periodt / On god / Say less / We move** — Punctuation phrases. End-of-statement emphasis. "I'm done discussing this."
- **Hits different** — Names that something feels uniquely special, not just "good." Wants to mark exception.
- **Cheugy / Mid / Based** — Taste verdicts. Cheugy = trying-too-hard, Mid = mediocre, Based = unapologetically yourself. All are positioning the speaker as having taste.
- **It's giving ___** — Channeling/comparing. "This thing has the energy of ___" — wants to evoke a vibe by reference.
- **Delulu** — Self-aware irrationality. Owning that they're being unrealistic, but doing it anyway. Wants permission to keep believing.
- **Rent free** — Naming intrusive thoughts. "I can't stop thinking about this and it's not even worth it."
- **Era** — Phase-marking. Treats a mood as a season of life. Lower-stakes than "I am a ___ person."
- **Pop off** — Permission/encouragement. "Go ahead, do the thing, I support you."
- **Crash out** — Naming public emotional collapse with a hint of admiration for the chaos.
- **Stan** — Naming devotion. "I am a fan of this person to a meaningful degree."
- **Moots** — Affection for online friends. Names a real connection that lives on socials.
- **Let him cook** — Patience signal. "Don't interrupt — they're building toward something."
- **Bed rotting** — Naming intentional rest as protest. Frames doing nothing as a choice, not failure.
- **Girl dinner / Girl math** — Naming feminine logic-with-a-wink. "I know this is irrational but I'm doing it anyway." Wants in-group laughs.
- **Deinfluencing** — Counter-consumption signal. "I'm telling you NOT to buy this" — moral positioning.
- **Sip tea / Spill / The tea** — Gossip role-claiming. Sip = observer, Spill = source.
- **Real / That's so real** — Validation. "I deeply relate."
- **Understood the assignment / brief / vision / moment / vibe** — Recognition of competence. Wants to register that someone *got it* and executed.
- **Ate / Ate that / Ate and left no crumbs** — Performance praise. Same idea — nailed it.
- **Cooked** — Dual: either "did it amazingly" OR "is completely ruined." Context decides.
- **We love to see it** — Approval, slightly distanced. Like clapping politely from a respectful distance.
- **Unalive** — TikTok-censorship workaround for "killed/dead." Wants to discuss heavy topics without algorithm punishment.
- **Choppelganger** — Self-deprecating humor about an unflattering lookalike. Wants laughs.
- **Aura / Aura farming** — Naming presence as if it were a stat. Wants to gamify charisma.

## VIETNAMESE → no English equivalent

### Gen X

- **Thời bao cấp** — Invoking shared hardship memory. "We lived through scarcity." Builds in-group bond and gives moral weight to current comfort.
- **Đổi Mới** — Generational landmark phrase. Says "I remember when everything changed." Pride + nostalgia.
- **Chạy xe ôm / Chạy chợ / Buôn bán / Cò** — Hustle-as-identity. Names a way of making money that's tied to street smarts. Wants the listener to recognize *resourcefulness*.
- **Chặt chém / Bị chặt** — Naming market predation. Either bragging about getting away with it, or warning about it.
- **Hàng xịn / Hàng nhái** — Authenticity binary. Quality is about *real vs. fake*, not premium vs. cheap.
- **Giang hồ** — Street-tough credibility. Wants to claim you've been around or know someone who has.
- **Số má** — Reputation flex. "People know my name." Quietly boastful.
- **Lo cho gia đình** — Moral identity. "Providing for family" isn't a chore — it's *who I am*. Wants respect.
- **Tự lực** — Self-reliance pride. Names it as a virtue, often when criticizing younger generations who didn't have to.
- **Khổ lắm** — Universal hardship marker. "Life has been heavy." Wants empathy without specifics.
- **Cực khổ / Vất vả** — Same as Khổ lắm but with different intensities of suffering.
- **Chơi đẹp / Chơi xấu** — Moral evaluation of behavior. "They played fair / dirty."
- **Hỗn / Láo / Vô lễ** — Generational disapproval of youth. Names a violation of the elder-respect order.
- **Xạo / Xạo ke** — Liar callout, casual.

### Millennials

- **Nhậu / Cụng ly / Dzô dzô / Trăm phần trăm** — Drinking ritual vocabulary. Each phrase performs a role in the bonding ceremony. Saying them = being a participant in male/group friendship culture.
- **Sến** — Naming over-romance. "This is too cheesy/melodramatic" but with affection — sến things are also kind of beloved.
- **Quê** — Class/style judgment. "Country, uncool, behind the times." Wants to position the speaker as urban-modern.
- **Chảnh / Chảnh chọe** — Calling out someone who's stuck-up. Often gendered (used about women more). Wants to deflate someone's social performance.
- **Điệu** — Naming overly elegant/dramatic affectation. "Don't be so extra."
- **Ổn áp** — Casual all-good signal. Less effort than full sentences. Wants to convey: "no drama, everything's fine."
- **Xịn / Xịn xò** — Quality praise. Names something as legit/premium. Wants to register approval of *quality*, not just liking.
- **Hợp gu / Bắt sóng** — Naming mutual understanding. "We click." Wants to acknowledge invisible compatibility.
- **La cà** — Aimless wandering acknowledgment. Confessing low productivity in a fond way.
- **Tám / Buôn chuyện** — Gossip permission. Saying "tám" claims the right to slow, indulgent chatting.
- **Ăn chơi** — Lifestyle declaration. "I'm spending money on fun." Slightly hedonistic, slightly proud.
- **Stress** — Borrowed word that carries real weight in VN Mil. Names overwhelm without the cultural baggage of older Vietnamese hardship language.

### Gen Z

- **U là tr** — Frustrated/exasperated prayer-shortening. "Oh my god / for crying out loud." The shortened spelling is half the joke.
- **VCL** — Strong reaction shorthand. "WTF" energy. Acronym keeps it texty/casual even when the feeling is intense.
- **J dz tr** — Confused-disbelief shortening. "What's even happening." The keyboard-style abbreviation = Gen Z signature.
- **Đm** — Strong informal curse. Used between close friends, signals comfort/familiarity.
- **Chằm Zn** — Joking depression. Says "I'm depressed" in code (Zn = kẽm = cảm → trầm cảm) so it's funny instead of heavy. Lets you mention dark feelings without truly committing to them.
- **Gòy soq** — "Welp, I'm cooked" with keyboard humor. The misspelling = the joke.
- **Mãi mận / Mãi keo / Mãi mận mãi keo** — Friendship oath. "Forever ride or die" with food/glue metaphors. Wants to claim a bond as permanent.
- **Bánh bèo** — Calling someone overly delicate/feminine. Slightly mocking, sometimes affectionate.
- **Lầy** — Naming playful stubbornness. "You're being a troublemaker" with a smile. Wants to register annoyance and amusement at once.
- **Xõa** — Permission to let loose. "Time to forget responsibilities." Wants release.
- **Cà khịa** — Playful teasing label. Pre-emptively says "I'm joking, don't take this seriously."
- **GATO** — Acronym-jealousy admission. Lighter than directly saying "I'm jealous." The acronym makes it cute.
- **Lemỏn** — Mocking someone who's stuck-up, with a wink. The accent on the "o" makes it feel ironic, not bitter.
- **Gét gô** — "Let's go!" performed with VN pronunciation = self-aware comedy.
- **Bó tay** — Surrender. "I literally cannot." Lighter than "I give up" — more like "I'm out of ideas."
- **Toang** — Naming complete failure with a shrug. "It's all fucked." Wants companionship in the disaster, not sympathy.
- **Chém gió** — Calling out exaggeration. "You're talking big." Affectionate accusation.
- **Sống ảo** — Naming fake-online-life. Often self-deprecating: "I'm posting curated nonsense and I know it."
- **Ăn hành** — "Eating onions" = taking criticism/a beating. Frames hardship as an absurd-funny meal you're being forced to eat.
- **Xu cà na** — Naming a bad-luck day. Wants companionship in the bad luck.
- **Đọc số tài khoản** — Joke reply to compliments. "Tell me your bank account number so I can pay you" = playful deflection.
- **Bóc trúng sít rịt** — Naming rare-bad-luck. Wants the listener to register how unfair the situation is.
- **Bà nói thiệt hả bà Thơ?** — "Are you serious, Ms. Tho?" — meme reference. Wants to invoke shared internet-culture knowledge.
- **Hi các mom** — Greeting everyone as "moms" — gender-neutral comedy. Wants warmth + jokes.
- **Hướng nội suốt phần đời còn lại** — "I'll be introverted forever now" after embarrassment. Self-deprecating. Wants laughs at their own expense.
- **Cơm nước gì chưa người đẹp?** — "Have you eaten yet, beautiful?" — affectionate-care greeting. Food as love language.
- **Cột sống** — Naming someone indispensable. "Spine" = "you're holding it all together for me."
- **Đỉnh của chóp** — Stacking superlatives. Like "GOAT of GOATs" — wants to register that "đỉnh" alone isn't enough.
- **Ngu vừa thôi** — "Cut it out, dummy" said with affection. Friend-language only.
- **Alo alo** — "Hello? You there?" — wants to gently bring someone back to the conversation.
- **Chết mất** — "I'm dying" (laughter or shock). Direct equivalent of "I'm dead" but with a slightly more *physical* feeling — chết = die, mất = vanish.

## How to use this in the app

When the AI hits one of these phrases and there's no direct translation:

1. Don't force a translation — **explain the move**.
2. Surface what the speaker is *trying to do emotionally*, not the literal words.
3. Example: User sends "I'm in my villain era" — instead of trying to translate "villain era" into Vietnamese (no equivalent), the AI shows: *"They're announcing they're done being nice. It's a phase, not a personality. Closest VN energy: claiming permission to be selfish without sounding cold."*

This is what makes Voeli different from Google Translate — translating the *intent*, not the words.
