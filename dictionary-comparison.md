# Voeli — English ↔ Vietnamese Cross-Language Comparison

For each generation: which phrases have a true cross-language equivalent, and which don't. "Untranslatable" means the concept itself doesn't exist or doesn't carry the same cultural weight in the other language. "Gap in your list" means an equivalent exists in real-world usage — you just haven't added it yet.

### Vietnamese Gen X → no English equivalent

**Untranslatable (culturally specific):**
- **Thời bao cấp** — the "subsidy era" memory has no US analog
- **Đổi Mới** — Vietnam's economic reform era, completely VN-specific

## 📊 Summary

| Generation | Strong matches | EN-only (untranslatable) | VN-only (untranslatable) | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gen X | ~5 | ~12 | ~10 | Lists describe **different lives** — US Gen X = mall/MTV; VN Gen X = market hustle/post-war |

### Biggest cultural gaps to flag in the app

**English concepts with no VN slang (most likely to confuse VN users):**
1. Soft launch / Hard launch / Situationship / Breadcrumbing — Western dating-app world
2. Roman Empire / Villain era / Main character / Soft life — TikTok identity framing
3. Therapy speak / Trauma dump / Gaslighting — wellness/therapy vocabulary
4. Touch grass / Bed rotting / Girl dinner — recent TikTok-era life framing
5. Quiet quitting / Touch base / Circle back — corporate-American work culture

**Vietnamese concepts with no EN slang (most likely to confuse EN users):**
1. Thời bao cấp / Đổi Mới — VN historical eras
2. Nhậu / Cụng ly / Dzô dzô — drinking-ritual culture
3. Sến / Quê / Chảnh — class/style/region judgment vocabulary
4. Chằm Zn / Gòy soq / Lemỏn — VN keyboard/spelling humor
5. Bóc trúng sít rịt / Xu cà na / Cột sống — VN-specific idioms

These are the spots where the app should probably **explain the concept** rather than try to translate it directly.
