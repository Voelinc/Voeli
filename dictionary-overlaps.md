For phrases that appear in multiple generations' lists, this picks **one primary owner** so the app gives a clean generation tag instead of "this phrase is everyone."

Rule of thumb: assign to the generation that **invented or popularized** it, not the ones who borrowed it later.

## Phrases I'm uncertain about — call your judgment

- **Yikes / Big yikes** — both feel Mil to me; Gen X variant might be just "Yikes" alone
- **Skinny ("give me the skinny")** — was Boomer-era; if anyone uses it now it's Gen X
- **Ratchet** — early Mil vs Gen Z hip-hop overlap

## Phrases that genuinely belong in two generations (keep dual-tagged)

These ones are real cross-generational shared vocabulary; forcing a single owner would feel wrong:
- **Stress** (Mil + Gen X VN) — both gens use it identically
- **Crush** (Mil VN + Gen Z VN) — borrowed at the same time
- **Sống ảo** (Mil + Gen Z VN) — Mil coined it, Gen Z still uses heavily
